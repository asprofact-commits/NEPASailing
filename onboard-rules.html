Good to know

Making yourself at home aboard

A handful of simple rules keep the yacht safe, clean and comfortable for everyone. None of it is fussy — it's just how we look after a boat that's loved and sailed every week.

Captain Theodoros will give you a short, friendly briefing when you step aboard. Here's what to expect, so you can relax from the moment you arrive.

👣

No shoes inside

Come aboard barefoot or in soft, light-soled, non-marking shoes only. Hard soles and street shoes scuff and damage the deck. Leave your shoes in the spot by the boarding ladder.

🏖️

Clear the sand after the beach

Back from a swim or a beach stop? Rinse your feet, towels and bags at the swim platform and brush off the sand before stepping back on deck. Sand scratches the teak and clogs the bilge.

🚽

The heads (toilet)

The yacht has marine heads. Please put nothing in the toilet that you haven't eaten first — only the paper provided — and follow the captain's quick flushing instructions to avoid blockages at sea.

🚭

Smoking

No smoking below deck. If you smoke, please do so only where the captain points you, downwind, and never throw anything overboard — we keep the sea clean.

🧴

Sun cream & oils

Oily creams stain cushions and decks. Let sun cream soak in before lying on the upholstery, and rinse off oils at the platform before settling into the cockpit.

🧺

Keep her tidy

Space is precious on a yacht. Stow your bags where the captain shows you, keep walkways clear, and we'll all move around safely and comfortably.

🦺

Safety first

Please follow the captain's instructions at all times, keep one hand for the boat when moving around, and wear a life jacket whenever the captain asks. Tell us in advance about non-swimmers.

🥂

Food & drinks

Enjoy yourself — drinks are welcome in moderation. Please use the cups provided rather than glass where possible, and secure drinks when we're under sail.

🐾

Children & pets

Children are very welcome but must be supervised at all times. Pets only by prior arrangement — just ask us when you book.

🌊

Respect the sea

Nothing goes overboard — we take all rubbish back to shore. In quiet anchorages, please keep the noise down in the evening out of respect for other boats.

What to bring

  • Swimwear, a towel and a light cover-up
  • Sun cream, sunglasses and a hat
  • A light jacket or layer for the breeze, especially on sunset cruises
  • Soft-soled shoes if you'd like (otherwise barefoot is perfect)
  • Any medication you need, and let us know about allergies in advance
