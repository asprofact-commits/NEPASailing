1. Our experiences

We offer two kinds of booking, and some conditions below apply to one or the other:

  • Daily cruises — private day trips, sunset cruises and private events that begin and end the same day from Saronida.
  • Multi-day charters — weekly or multi-day yacht charters, where the yacht is taken for a longer trip across the Saronic Gulf and beyond.

2. Bookings

A booking is requested by WhatsApp, phone, email or our website and becomes confirmed only once we have acknowledged availability and received the required deposit or down payment. Until then, no date is held. The person making the booking must be at least 18 years old and is responsible for the whole party.

3. Prices & what's included

Daily cruise prices are quoted per trip and confirmed at booking. They normally include the captain, fuel for the planned route, fresh water, basic drinks, freshly cooked food on board and standard safety equipment. Extras such as special menus, premium drinks, extra hours or transfers are quoted separately.

Multi-day charter prices are quoted in Euro and are typically €1,500 – €3,500 per week, depending on season, duration and route. For multi-day charters, fuel and berthing/marina fees are paid by the charterer (see charter conditions below). Prices shown on the website are indicative starting prices and may vary.

4. Discounts

Discounts may be combined up to a maximum total of 10%:

  • 10% early-booking discount (dates announced each season);
  • 10% for winter charters;
  • 5% on two-week charters;
  • 5% for repeat customers, or for two bookings in the same season.

5. Charter packet (multi-day)

For multi-day charters, a small but mandatory service fee for the Charter Packet (€200) is payable at the base. It includes:

  • linen & towels;
  • gas bottles;
  • dinghy;
  • marina fee for the first and last day (€50);
  • final cleaning (€150).

6. Charter conditions (multi-day)

  • All prices quoted for multi-day charters are valid in Euro for one week.
  • The yacht is normally delivered on Saturday at 17:00 and must be returned the following Saturday by 09:00. The yacht must be back in the home bay the evening before, by 19:00.
  • The water tank is filled by our crew free of charge.
  • Fuel and berthing fees are at the charterer's expense. The fuel tanks must be returned full.
  • At least one valid sailing licence is required for a bareboat charter.
  • A list of participants, a copy of the licence and your arrival time must be submitted at least one week before the yacht is delivered.

7. Payment & deposit

  • To secure your booking, a 40% down payment is required.
  • The balance (the remaining 60% of the charter fee) is due two weeks before embarkation.
  • A security deposit of €2,000 is required for multi-day charters, payable at the base prior to embarkation by credit card (Visa or Mastercard) or in cash. It is returned after the yacht is handed back in good order.
  • Down payments and balances are paid by bank transfer unless otherwise agreed.

8. Cancellation policy

Daily cruises. A full refund is given if the cruise has to be cancelled due to bad weather and cannot be rescheduled. If you cancel for your own reasons, please give as much notice as possible so we can offer you another date.

Multi-day charters.

  • Cancelled 46 days or more before embarkation: 50% of the total charter fee is retained.
  • Cancelled less than 45 days before embarkation: no refund.
  • No-show: every amount paid is withheld.

We strongly recommend travel-cancellation insurance to cover these situations (see Insurance below).

9. Weather

Your safety always comes first. If the captain decides that wind, sea state or other conditions make a trip unsafe, we may shorten, reschedule or cancel it. For daily cruises that cannot be rescheduled, your payment is refunded in full. The decision on whether conditions are safe rests solely with the captain and is final. We are not liable for incidental costs (such as travel or accommodation) arising from a weather cancellation.

10. Insurance & liability

  • The chartered yacht and its equipment are insured against fire, sea damage, risks of collision, third-party liability and total loss.
  • Damage caused by gross negligence or wilful acts is excluded from cover.
  • For multi-day charters, the charterer is in principle only liable up to the amount of the security deposit (€2,000).
  • You can protect that deposit by taking out a deposit (bonding) insurance, so that the paid deposit is itself insured. Note that gross negligence, wilful acts, lost equipment, items going overboard and simple theft are typically excluded.
  • We consider a deposit insurance and/or an extended skipper third-party liability insurance essential, and recommend a combined skipper & crew package, as well as travel-cancellation insurance. We are happy to point you to a suitable policy.
  • To the fullest extent permitted by law, we are not liable for indirect or consequential losses, or for injury, loss or damage arising from a guest's failure to follow the captain's instructions, from pre-existing medical conditions, or from circumstances beyond our reasonable control (force majeure). Nothing in these terms limits liability that cannot be limited under Greek law.

11. Safety & conduct on board

The captain is responsible for the vessel and everyone aboard at all times, and all guests must follow the captain's instructions without exception. The captain may refuse boarding to, or end the trip for, any guest who is intoxicated, behaves dangerously, or refuses to follow safety instructions, without refund. Life jackets are available on board and must be worn when the captain requires it. Please also read our Onboard Rules, which help keep the yacht safe and comfortable for everyone.
